About

The Imageomics Institute, headquartered at The Ohio State University, is pioneering a new field of science at the intersection of biology and data. By harnessing advances in machine learning and vast collections of biological images, Imageomics transforms pictures of organisms into computable traits that deepen our understanding of life's diversity. From unlocking the secrets of species' form and function to building open tools and resources for the global research community, the institute is training the next generation of scientists and creating a vibrant, collaborative ecosystem where biology and data science converge to drive discovery.

Mission

"Creating a collaborative research, training, and community-facing environment for extracting existing and new biological traits from images of organisms, with the necessary infrastructure for cyber, information, and model development."

Vision

"To establish a new scientific field called imageomics that harnesses revolutions in data science and computing, as well as the rapidly expanding collections of biological image data, in order to accelerate biological understanding of phenotypic traits extracted from images of organisms."
